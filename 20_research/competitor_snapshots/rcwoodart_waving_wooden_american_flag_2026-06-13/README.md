## Visual Positioning Notes

- The primary product look is glossy, high-grain, red/blue stained wood with natural wood stripes rather than opaque white stripes.
- Stars appear carved or engraved into the wood and left natural/light against the blue field.
- Product photos strongly emphasize thickness and dimensional wave depth; side-angle photos matter because the product is not just a flat graphic.
- Scale ladder image is important: the offer makes more sense when all five sizes are seen together.
- The product reads as rustic patriotic decor rather than minimalist flag art.
- Brick and pale-wood backgrounds reinforce handmade/rustic positioning.
- Gloss reflections are visible in several images, so finish choice may need clear expectation setting if Drakkar offers matte/satin/natural alternatives.

## Competitive Analysis

- The size ladder creates a good-better-best structure from a $50 XSM natural option to a $450 XL finished option.
- The page uses the XL at $450 as a high anchor while still leaving shippable/lower-ticket entries for gift buyers.
- Natural finish being $10 cheaper is simple and consistent, but the page does not explain the labor/material difference.
- Service-member and first-responder discounts reinforce the patriotic/customer-affinity angle and may increase trust with the target audience.
- Early-bird gift discount is operationally useful: it trains customers to order ahead for seasonal demand instead of expecting rush production.
- The lack of included hardware is a possible friction point; bundling ready-to-hang hardware could be a Drakkar differentiator if cost and installation time are small.
- Nationwide shipping is part of the offer, but the page does not expose packaging method, lead time, finished weight, or damage-risk handling.
- No visible customer reviews were captured on the product page.
- No exact lead time was captured on the product page.
- No material species was captured beyond solid wood.

## Drakkar Implications

- Existing internal wavy-flag build research should be treated as build-method reference, while this page is market/positioning reference.
- The Wood Doofus build note reports a 24 in. x 13 in. flag selling locally around $100; this RC Wood Art MED option is 23 in. x 12.25 in. and listed at $130-$140. That suggests the $100 local benchmark may be conservative for a higher-finish version, but local demand and finish quality need verification.
- The WhosTheVoss build note centers on an approximately 37 in. x 19.5 in. flag. This page's LG option is 35 in. x 19.5 in. at $230-$240, giving a useful finished-product comp near that size.
- A Drakkar version should not be approved until there is a cost sheet covering lumber, finish, stars/stencil/engraving time, clear coat, hardware, packaging, labor, and delivery/shipping risk.
- If pursued, a first test could be framed as a seasonal/patriotic product experiment rather than a core cedar-garden catalog item.
- Photo planning should include: all-size lineup if multiple sizes exist, wall-mounted angle, side-thickness/wave-detail view, close-up of stars, finish-detail close-up, and one clean scale cue.
- Customer-copy generation would need Claude per repo governance. This note is prep/research only.

## Open Questions Before Product Setup

- What local material and finish cost would Drakkar have for each size?
- Would Drakkar build from pine/construction lumber, cedar, or another stock?
- Can Drakkar reproduce hand-engraved/carved stars efficiently and consistently?
- What is the true labor time by size, including carving, star work, finish, and cure time?
- Should hanging hardware be included by default?
- Is the item local-only, shippable, or offered in small sizes for shipping and large sizes for pickup/delivery?
- Is there enough seasonal demand outside July 4th, Veterans Day, Memorial Day, Christmas, and first-responder gift occasions?
