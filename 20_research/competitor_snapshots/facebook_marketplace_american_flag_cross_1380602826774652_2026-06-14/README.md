- Raised wood stars are natural/burned wood rather than white painted stars.
- Stripe treatment alternates red-stained blocks with burned/natural wood blocks.

## Competitive Analysis

- At $30 each, or $25 each for multiple units, this is a much lower-ticket patriotic item than the wavy/draped flag listings.
- The bulk-price language creates a simple local-gift or event-favor angle.
- The faith-and-patriotism positioning is narrower than standard flag decor but may be strong for local gift buyers.
- The listing leaves major operational details unstated: dimensions, finish, hanging hardware, lead time, and shipping/pickup.
- The same seller's $100 draped flag gives this listing a complementary add-on role in a small patriotic decor cluster.

## Drakkar Implications

- Treat this as a possible low-ticket add-on/product-test comp, not as a direct substitute for large wavy flags.
- A Drakkar version would require a product-family decision because it introduces explicit faith positioning.
- If pursued, the multi-unit price model is worth testing for gift buyers, events, and local pickup bundles.
- A Drakkar listing would need exact dimensions, hanging method, finish details, and batch-variation expectations.
- Product photography should include front, wall-mounted, scale/handheld, group/batch, and back-side/hardware views.
- Customer-copy generation would need Claude per repo governance. This note is prep/research only.

## Open Questions Before Product Setup

- What are the exact dimensions and weight?
- Is hanging hardware included by default?
- What wood stock and finish are used?
- Is the product intended for indoor use only, or can it tolerate humidity/outdoor exposure?
- How much labor does the raised-star and burned-stripe layout require at batch scale?
- Would Drakkar want to carry faith-forward patriotic items, or keep the catalog secular/patriotic only?
- Could the $25 multi-unit price be profitable after materials, labor, packaging, and Marketplace messaging time?
