## Competitive Analysis

- The listing shares the same size/finish price ladder as the non-police wavy flag variants, suggesting the Thin Blue Line colorway is not priced as a premium upgrade.
- The law-enforcement niche gives the product stronger gift intent than a generic blacked-out flag, especially for graduations, service recognition, memorials, birthdays, and holidays.
- The visible personalization/memorial image could increase emotional value, but it also creates operational risk if buyers expect custom names or phrases without a clear paid option.
- The tag set is narrower and highly police/Thin Line focused; broad wall-decor and general gift tags from the standard flag listing are absent.
- The size-lineup image gives better buying confidence than listings that only show one size, because the wavy flags vary strongly by scale.
- Hardware not included remains a possible friction point; including ready-to-hang hardware could be a Drakkar differentiator if cost and install time are manageable.
- No visible customer reviews were captured on the product page.
- No exact lead time was captured on the product page.
- No material species was captured beyond solid wood.
- No finished weight, packaging method, or shipping damage policy was captured.

## Drakkar Implications

- Existing internal wavy-flag build research should be treated as build-method reference, while this page is market/positioning reference.
- A Thin Blue Line version would need intentional product-positioning review before Drakkar adopts it, because it is affinity/service-symbol decor rather than a neutral patriotic flag variant.
- If offered, personalization/memorial text must be governed as a separate approved customization path with exact text, placement, customer proofing, and pricing.
- The blue stripe should be treated as a design-critical feature; photos and QC should verify that it remains visible but not cartoon-bright.
- Black/blue finish consistency, gloss reflection, carved-star contrast, and clear-coated surface quality would be important quality-control risks.
- If Drakkar tests this, the product record should separate the base Thin Blue Line configuration from any custom memorial/name/phrase option.

## Open Questions Before Product Setup

- What local material and finish cost would Drakkar have for each size?
- Would Drakkar build from pine/construction lumber, cedar, or another stock?
- Does Drakkar want to offer police/Thin Blue Line affinity decor at all?
- How much labor does black/blue finish work add compared with the standard and non-police blacked-out versions?
- Can Drakkar reproduce hand-engraved/carved stars efficiently and consistently?
- Is personalization or memorial text part of the offer, and if so what proofing, pricing, and approval path governs it?
- What is the true labor time by size, including carving, star work, burning, blue-line layout, black finish, clear coat, customization, and cure time?
- Should hanging hardware be included by default?
- Is the item local-only, shippable, or offered in small sizes for shipping and large sizes for pickup/delivery?
- Is there enough seasonal and service-recognition demand outside July 4th, Veterans Day, Memorial Day, Christmas, police graduations, retirements, and memorial occasions?
