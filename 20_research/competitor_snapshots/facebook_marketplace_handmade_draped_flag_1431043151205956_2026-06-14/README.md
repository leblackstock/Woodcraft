- The $100 price is lower than Belinda Gibbons's $200 draped option and much lower than RC Wood Art's larger Shopify wavy-flag ladder.
- The listing relies on a simple one-price offer rather than a size ladder, which may reduce friction for a local impulse buy.
- The product concept is more sculptural and novelty/gift-oriented than the wall-mounted wavy flags.
- Booth-style photos make the seller look active in small-batch local sales, but the listing does not document dimensions, materials, hardware, or lead time.
- The seller's related American Flag Cross creates a small patriotic decor cluster rather than a one-off flag listing.

## Drakkar Implications

- Treat this as a local-market comp for a lower-ticket draped/folded flag concept.
- A Drakkar version would need exact dimensions, base/stand or hanging behavior, finish details, and stability notes before any listing work.
- The $100 local price may be a useful test anchor if labor is low and the item can be batched.
- If outdoor use is claimed, Drakkar should specify finish system, intended exposure, maintenance expectations, and warranty boundaries.
- Photo planning would need both a clean hero image and a scale/stability image; booth photos are useful proof of inventory but not enough for polished listing copy.
- Customer-copy generation would need Claude per repo governance. This note is prep/research only.

## Open Questions Before Product Setup

- What are the exact dimensions and weight?
- Is it designed to stand on its own, hang on a wall, or both?
- What wood species or stock is used?
- What finish is used, and is outdoor use truly supported?
- Can the design be batched efficiently enough to support a $100 local price?
- Is this a craft-show product, made-to-order Marketplace product, or both?
