## Drakkar Implications

- Existing internal wavy-flag build research should be treated as build-method reference, while this page is market/positioning reference.
- A blacked-out finish could be a separate finish/colorway option if Drakkar tests wavy flags, but it should not be bundled into the base product without labor and material proof.
- This finish may appeal to buyers who want a less bright patriotic decor piece, first-responder adjacent decor, or darker room/garage/shop styling.
- Black/charcoal finish consistency, dust visibility, gloss reflection, and clear-coat quality would be important quality-control risks.
- If Drakkar offers a blacked-out version, the product record should specify whether red/blue symbolism is intentionally removed, muted, or replaced by black/natural contrast.
- Photo planning should include side-thickness/wave-detail views, star close-ups, blackened-side detail, a wall-mounted lifestyle image, and one straight-on shot for thumbnail clarity.
- Customer-copy generation would need Claude per repo governance. This note is prep/research only.

## Open Questions Before Product Setup

- What local material and finish cost would Drakkar have for each size?
- Would Drakkar build from pine/construction lumber, cedar, or another stock?
- How much labor does blacking out/staining add compared with the standard and extra-rustic red/blue versions?
- Can Drakkar reproduce hand-engraved/carved stars efficiently and consistently?
- What is the true labor time by size, including carving, star work, burning, black finish, clear coat, and cure time?
- Should blacked-out, extra-rustic, and standard red/blue be separate product records or finish variants under one wavy-flag product family?
- Should hanging hardware be included by default?
- Is the item local-only, shippable, or offered in small sizes for shipping and large sizes for pickup/delivery?
- Is there enough seasonal demand outside July 4th, Veterans Day, Memorial Day, Christmas, and first-responder gift occasions?
