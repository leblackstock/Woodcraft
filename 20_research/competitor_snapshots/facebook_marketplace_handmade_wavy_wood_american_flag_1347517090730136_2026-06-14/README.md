## Competitive Analysis

- The local Marketplace price ladder is much lower than the RC Wood Art Shopify ladder for comparable wavy flag concepts: $75 small, $150 medium, $275 large, and $200 draped.
- The listing uses one ambiguous headline price but relies on description text to explain size/options; this is common on Marketplace but creates buyer friction.
- Including mounting hardware is a practical differentiator versus competitors that charge extra or require customers to ask.
- "Custom made to order" allows flexible fulfillment but leaves unanswered lead time, exact dimensions, finish options, and pickup/shipping terms.
- The seller appears to be operating a narrow offer at capture time: the seller profile showed only one active listing, and no other similar seller listings were visible.
- "Spar urethane" and indoor/outdoor positioning can broaden use cases, but outdoor suitability would need very careful expectation setting if Drakkar offered a similar item.

## Drakkar Implications

- Treat this as a local Marketplace pricing comp, not as a polished ecommerce listing comp.
- The $75/$150/$275 ladder is useful for local-market sensitivity, especially against the RC Wood Art $110-$450 Shopify range.
- Drakkar should avoid a vague single headline price if offering multiple sizes; use size-specific price rows and exact dimensions.
- If Drakkar includes hanging hardware by default, call that out as a concrete customer benefit.
- Any Drakkar outdoor-use claim should specify finish system, intended exposure level, maintenance expectations, and warranty boundaries.
- Customer-copy generation would need Claude per repo governance. This note is prep/research only.
