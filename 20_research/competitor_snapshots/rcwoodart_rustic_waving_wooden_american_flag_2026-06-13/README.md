- This variant is visually distinct from the standard waving flag through heavy torching/burning on the natural stripes.
- Red stripes remain stained, but the alternating lighter stripes carry dark burn bands and high grain contrast.
- The blue canton is glossy and translucent enough for wood grain to show through.

## Competitive Analysis

- The same price ladder as the standard listing suggests the store treats extra-rustic burning as a style variant rather than a premium-priced upgrade.
- The extra-rustic look creates stronger visual differentiation than finish selection alone, especially in thumbnail/search contexts.
- The product title includes both `Extra Rustic` and `Wood Burnt`, which makes the finish mode clear before opening the description.
- The JSON tag set is narrower than the standard waving flag listing. It focuses more on flag/rustic/police/thin-line terms and omits many broad gift, wall decor, living room, and holiday tags captured on the standard listing.
- The `We the People` imagery could widen buyer appeal for patriotic decor but also adds a text/customization expectation that should be made explicit if Drakkar ever offers a similar option.
- Hardware not included remains a possible friction point; including ready-to-hang hardware could be a Drakkar differentiator if the cost and install time are manageable.

## Drakkar Implications

- Existing internal wavy-flag build research should be treated as build-method reference, while this page is market/positioning reference.
- The rustic/burnt finish should be costed separately from the standard stained/natural-stripe finish because torching adds time, safety setup, surface cleanup, and consistency risk.
- If Drakkar tests wavy flags, the rustic finish could be a distinct SKU/finish option rather than a default, especially if labor time is materially higher.
- The same-price competitor ladder means Drakkar should not assume buyers will pay a premium for burn treatment without local proof.
- A `We the People` option should be treated as its own approved configuration with explicit text, placement, and customer-copy governance, not as an incidental photo detail.
- Photo planning should include side-thickness/wave-detail views, close-ups of burned stripe texture, close-ups of carved stars, one clean non-text version, and one text-option version if offered.
- Customer-copy generation would need Claude per repo governance. This note is prep/research only.

## Open Questions Before Product Setup

- What local material and finish cost would Drakkar have for each size?
- Would Drakkar build from pine/construction lumber, cedar, or another stock?
- How much additional labor and safety setup does torching add compared with the standard stained/natural-stripe version?
- Can Drakkar reproduce hand-engraved/carved stars efficiently and consistently?
- What is the true labor time by size, including carving, star work, burning, finishing, and cure time?
- Is `We the People` a desired option, and if so what exact text, font/style, placement, and approval path should govern it?
- Should hanging hardware be included by default?
- Is the item local-only, shippable, or offered in small sizes for shipping and large sizes for pickup/delivery?
- Is there enough seasonal demand outside July 4th, Veterans Day, Memorial Day, Christmas, and first-responder gift occasions?
