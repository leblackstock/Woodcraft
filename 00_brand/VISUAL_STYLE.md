Use this direction for brand-specific graphics, generated visuals, ads, HTML, templates, and image prompts. It should keep branded work consistent with the Drakkar catalog while still letting product photos feel warm, natural, and useful.

## Preferred Feel

- Keep the look cedar-forward, practical, local, warm, and unfussy.
- Match the approved colors in `00_brand/COLOR_PALETTE.md`, especially black/charcoal, warm stone, muted silver, and cedar/burnt orange.
- Do not force every image to be very dark. Dark brand cards are useful, but lifestyle and product images can use daylight, porch warmth, greenery, stone/cream backgrounds, and visible cedar color.
- Keep product wood warm and cedar-forward, with realistic grain and a handmade feel.
- Use dark/charcoal backgrounds mainly for price cards, brand/order cards, or dramatic hero composites.
- Use light stone/cream backgrounds when a cleaner or more readable image works better.
- Avoid bright unrelated colors, neon palettes, glossy luxury styling, generic stock-photo blues, and washed-out beige that loses the Drakkar identity.

## Reference Image Notes

The approved visual reference includes:

- black/charcoal brand panel
- warm cream typography
- burnt-orange cedar accent
- realistic cedar planter as the main product signal
- natural greenery and porch/garden context
- local handmade positioning
- structured graphic layout with readable text

Use the reference as an inspiration target, not a requirement that every image be equally dark or text-heavy.

## Marketplace Application

- Marketplace photo slots should answer distinct shopper questions and should not become repeated ad cards.
- Use daylight, stone/cream, greenery, and warm cedar when they sell the product better than a dark branded composition.
- Reserve strong brand panels and heavier typography mainly for price, dimensions/details, important-details, and ordering graphics.
- Keep readable requested text in image prompts when the workflow calls for it.
